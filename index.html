Nature-based learning and playful care for curious children.

Soil and Sky grew out of years in classrooms and public schools—where schedules stay full and screens often show up as a prize. That setup can quiet a moment, but it rarely settles the feelings underneath. Here, the day is slower, smaller, and rooted outside.

Children explore, move, tell stories, and get their hands dirty while learning how the natural world works: the trees, the clouds, where water goes, how food nourishes us, and how we care for our space together. The rhythm draws on Waldorf and Montessori-inspired ideas—warm structure, not a rigid script.

No big center. No screens as the default “treat.”
Just patient, structured playtime with someone who is really teaching—not only watching.

Ways to work with Soil and Sky

Small groups, one-on-one care, and event support with a nature-first curriculum you can see and talk about at pickup.

Nature Learning Sessions

Small-group outdoor learning for 1 to 4 children.

These three-hour sessions are for 1–4 children to explore, move, create, and learn through nature-based play—seasonal crafts, scavenger hunts, stories, sensory play, walks, and group games tied to what’s alive outside.

Families receive a written recap and nature-learning themes after our time together, so you know what we touched on and how to keep the thread going at home.

Best for: families who want recurring or occasional enrichment with clear communication home.

One-on-One Nature Play

Personalized outdoor play and enrichment for individual families.

For parents who are stretched thin—this is the extra hands that still teach. We might make lunch together, sing cleanup songs, practice kind words, or build tiny routines (even silly ones) that help with transitions like potty time—always through play, never through shame.

Some children simply do better one-on-one or in a very small pod than in a loud center. If big settings have been hard on your child, we can move at their pace outdoors.

Best for: families who want individualized attention, gentle structure, and a partner in outdoor learning—not “just a babysitter.”

Event Childcare with a Nature-Learning Twist

Hosting a birthday party, wedding, family gathering, or outdoor event?

Soil and Sky provides attentive child support with playful activities that keep children engaged, safe, and happy while adults enjoy the event.

Activities may include nature crafts, movement games, story circles, scavenger hunts, sensory play, and quiet reset activities.

Best for: birthdays, weddings, family parties, and community gatherings.

Why Nature-Based Play Matters

Children need room to move, wonder, and discover.

So much of childhood now lives indoors, on a clock, and within reach of a tablet. When screens become the main “reward,” it can be harder—not easier—for children to settle when the device goes away. Soil and Sky offers a different rhythm: body first, wonder first, then rest.

Outside, children get room to regulate big feelings, try again, and take pride in what they do, not only what they watch. That builds confidence, language, empathy, and problem-solving in a way that sticks—because it’s connected to real mud, real friends, and real tasks.

The goal is simple: more fresh air, more kindness, more movement, and play that earns its keep.
