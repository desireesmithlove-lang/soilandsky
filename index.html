What Soil and Sky Is

Nature-based learning and playful care for curious kids.

Soil and Sky is a flexible outdoor learning experience for children. Through seasonal activities, child-led exploration, movement, storytelling, and hands-on play, kids are invited to slow down, get curious, and build a meaningful connection with the natural world.

No big center. No screens. No pressure.
Just thoughtful time outside with a caring guide.

Nature Learning Sessions

Small-group outdoor learning for 1 to 4 children.

These three-hour sessions are designed for children to explore, move, create, observe, and learn through nature-based play. Activities may include seasonal crafts, scavenger hunts, storytelling, sensory play, nature walks, and imaginative group games.

Best for: families looking for recurring or occasional screen-free enrichment.

One-on-One Nature Play

Personalized outdoor play and enrichment for individual families.

This option is for parents who want their child to spend more time outside, build confidence, and engage in creative, child-led exploration with warm support.

Best for: families who want individualized attention, gentle structure, and outdoor learning without a formal classroom setting.

Event Childcare with a Nature-Learning Twist

Hosting a birthday party, wedding, family gathering, or outdoor event?

Soil and Sky provides attentive child support with playful activities that keep kids engaged, safe, and happy while adults enjoy the event.

Activities may include nature crafts, movement games, story circles, scavenger hunts, sensory play, and quiet reset activities.

Best for: birthdays, weddings, family parties, and community gatherings.

Why Nature-Based Play Matters

Children need room to move, wonder, and discover.

So much of modern childhood happens indoors, scheduled, and on screens. Soil and Sky offers a gentle alternative.

Nature-based play helps children build confidence, imagination, emotional regulation, problem-solving skills, and a deeper sense of connection with the world around them.

The goal is simple: more fresh air, more curiosity, more movement, and more meaningful play.
